a day in nostr

blurred

2023.jan.26

It's a night with clear skies. Twinkling stars spread across the darkness like decentralized relays. If only we could gaze at the starry night of nostr.

Everyday we open a window to peer through conversations, waving what seems to be a flag of FOMO into the nostr cyberspace. But is it really FOMO?

While, in other platforms, we may be distracted by ads and click-bait headlines that bury free human thought and conversation, today, we open our mobile phones to human connection, at nostr.

What irony that today was a day of porn spam. Like a wave, it flooded the global feeds across nostr. In any other platform we may have heard outrage. Nostr, however, holds a different spirit...it begins with people asking what can we do about this?

If you've been around nostr long enough, it is a place to openly share challenges and possible solutions. It is a haven for builders. Today, many were grateful that Damus blurs images of people you do not follow, without a doubt, soon to come to a nostr client near you.

Nostr spammed back the spammers with a good number of jokes. Perhaps if it was some kind of a battle, I would say the jokes have won over spam. Even if spam outnumbered jokes, it was but noise compared to the high signal of the nostr community. Is the nostr community giving us noise cancelling headphones, even as spam scrolls past us on the global feed? I'd like to think that it is. That somehow, nostr strengthens how we live our lives, because we choose to be stronger in the face of challenges, and laugh about it along the way. We may never end spam, but we can end its hold on us.

As I close off for the night, you'll probably get this tomorrow (depending where you are in the world), perhaps it will arrive like a newspaper tossed to your nostr front door.
