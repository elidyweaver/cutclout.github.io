post.14

How do you prove you're real?

Wed Feb 8, 2023

Each human being is so unique and creative in their own way, that, it is hard to fathom anyone wanting to be an impostor. Impostors exist, and it can be a challenge to know the difference.

If you know someone in real life, it is easy to exchange your npubs, but what happens when you don't?

In nostr you can tie your account to your website as a way of verifying you identity through what's called a NIP05. In some nostr products, you can see your name with an @yourwebsite.com This is particularly useful for content creators, proving you're the author, or you belong to that organization.

To help tell you apart from bots, some websites offer NIP05's. It doesn't end with your domain, it ends with theirs, but it is still one strung higher in the ladder of trust.

Combine that with other trust signals to reveal that unique mixture that makes you, you: Your interactions with the community, creating and sharing your own content, sharing what you find interesting, in other words, just being you is a trust signal.

What makes you who you are?

post.13

My handle is available?

Tues Feb 7, 2023

If we had to use unique names in real life, like we do in most platforms, perhaps we would have strange lengthy names. Our names are not unique. They're often short and meaningful to our families in some way. In our lifetimes we meet other people with the same name.

Nostr lets us have whichever name we want. It solves for this need of uniqueness in an unexpected way. When you first use nostr, you get a pair of unique long strings of numbers. One of them is your npub or public key. When you share your npub with someone, they find your account. They can also tag you with it @YourNpub

However, you do not see the long string of numbers when they tag you, you see your handle. Oh the magic of nostr!

You can choose the handle you want. Others can use that handle too, but you are still uniquely you, and so is your npub.

post.12

You're the keeper of your secret key

Mon Feb 6, 2023

Used to the ways of platforms, you may think someone can help you regain your password. When a platform owns your account, it knows your password and hence can help you recover it.

When a platform owns your account, it can also shut it down. One day you're in, the next you're out. They control it.

In nostr, you're the one in control.

You need to keep your secret or private key (nsec), the equivalent of a password but for nostr, stored safely. No one can help you recover it, because you own it, along with your identity, posts, list of followers...

In the future, we may see options to save an encrypted copy in the cloud, perhaps some options already exist. In the meantime, keep it safe in a password manager.

If you're logging into web nostr clients, also use a browser extension.
