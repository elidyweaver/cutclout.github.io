nostr 101

the nostrverse keeps growing
recommendations here may have different results based on which client you choose

.

Nostr gives you control & choice of your social media experience+

Your notesinstead of tweets or posts, nostr has notes belong to you. You also keep your follows and followers, no matter which nostr clienta nostr client is an app or website created with nostr code you use. No one can close down your account, though people could mute you and you could mute them.

Step 1

Choose a nostr client to try.

Nostr is a growing universe of clients that are expanding beyond what you know of social media. We're starting to see blogging, marketplaces, music...

Nostr is a protocol, which means its code can be used to create many different types of apps and websites. You can log into most of them with one account. Think of it like being able to enter either Facebook and Twitter, and have your identity in both of them, regardless of which one you use. Anyone could find you on either of them, and you could login to both of them and find your same notes, follows, followers...

Nostr is a protocol, not a platform.

.

Step 2

Get your unique pair of keys.

You don't need to give up your email or phone number to join. Instead, the first time you sign into a nostr client, you get a special set of keys (long strings of numbers unique to you) that you can use to enter most of the nostr universe.

Your public key:

npub + LongStringOfNumbersUniqueToYou

Share your public key with your friends. It takes the place of your handle, which means you can pick whichever handle you want.

Copy it from your profile (often found by a key icon )

If you spot a QR icon in your nostr client, others will get your npub by scanning it and open up your profile page.

You are the keeper of your secret or private key:

nsec + LongStringOfNumbersUniqueToYou

This is a special kind of password. You can use it to sign in to nostr clients, and to create notes.

It belongs only to you, not to a company. If you are used to the ways of platforms like Twitter and Facebook, you may think someone can help you recover it, but you won't find a "forgot your password?" option.

Nostr is different, you're the one in control. When someone owns your account, they know your password and can help you recover it. One of the tradeoffs is that they can also close down your account. At nostr you own your account and should keep your nsec safe.

TIP: Save it in a password manager (for example Bitwarden).
Some nostr clients keep your secret key hidden in the Settings. Make sure you copy it and save it in your password manager before logging out.
In the future, we may see options to save an encrypted copy in the cloud, perhaps some options already exist.

If you are logging in through a website (not an app), save your nostr nsec in a special browser extension first. Browsers are not as safe, your secret key could get stolen. You may see this option to log in labelled as "login with extension or NIP-07"

Not your keys, not your social media.

.

Step 3

Add some relays, perhaps 8 is a magic number!?

Notes are spread out unto cyberspace through servers running nostr software, called relays.

Comprehensive relay list: nostr.watch

Anyone who's a little tech savvy could run a relay. Relays don't belong to a single company, and they do not own your account.

The nostr relays keep your information and share it with other relays. Adding a few relays to your nostr profile is a way to make sure your information is still available even if a relay were to go down.

Some nostr clients add a set of relays automatically, others suggest them based on the people you follow. Some relays are paid and they are likely to have less spam.

TIP: Use a VPN service to protect your IP (like Mullvad). Nostr does not gather and sell your information like other platforms, but relays can know your IP.

Relays help make nostr decentralized & censorship resistant

.

Step 4

Make your first note

#nostrwelcome & #grownostr are good hashtags to use for your first note. You could say hello world, hello nostr, or testing, but it may make you sound like spam, and some relays try to protect against that.

How do you want to remember your first post? Whatever you choose, make it you.

If you aren't following anyone yet and your nostr client does not have a universal feed, you may feel like you are writing into the void. You are not. Nostr is listening.

Step 5

What's in a photograph?

Images are a way of expressing so much...from sunrises and sunsets to memories and memes. If you've been looking for the upload image button, you may find it's missing.

One of nostr's strengths is that it does not rely on a single central server service to keep everyone's notes. Many people should be able to host a server (relay), even if only to keep personal notes, in order to prevent the biases and choices of one central server from applying rules to everyone, like who is allowed to use nostr.

If saving people's notes can take a lot of room in a server over time, imagine how much more room saving images or videos might take. To keep nostr light, instead of uploading your images to nostr, you need to upload them to another website first, copy the URL, and then paste it in your note. You may use services like nostr.build or nostrimg.com

If you're using Damus, you may find it has a work around. You'll find an image upload button, and it will upload it to a website like nostr.build (based on your settings), copy the URL and paste it into your note.

.

Step 6

Mentions & Quotes (Renotes)

Method 1

Add an "@" symbol before an npub or noteID
Mention: @npub
Quote: @noteID

Method 2

Add "nostr:" before an npub or noteID
Mention: nostr:npub
Quote: nostr:noteID

Once you send your note, many nostr clients will display a person's handle when mentioning them by their npub and will let you hover over it to see a preview of their profile. Some only do that if you follow the people mentioned.

If you quote a note by its ID, you should be able to see it display below your thoughts. If all you see is that long string of numbers, make sure you try both methods, and that you hit enter after pasting an npub or noteID. If you still only see the long string of numbers, it may be that the nostr client you are using does not support quoting notes. It's only a matter of time until it does.

You can copy an npub from profile pages, and a noteID from menus directly on notes. Since more than one person can have the same handle, it is a good idea to follow the people you know are real. As a plus, if you follow them, some nostr clients will allow you to tag them more easily by typing their handle.

.

Step 7

Discover your niche

Nostr is an extension of our social lives. As our thoughts flow freely on the keyboard and unto our nostr feeds, they meet readers on the other side. A note can become the start of a conversation. Threads are weaved, moments are shared, a community is born.

As you start following people, you will see their posts in real time on your home tab. You can discover more people by seeing who your friends are following, mentioning and re-noting.

Some nostr clients let you search for hashtags and have recommended lists of people you can follow.

For finding friends you know from Twitter, or friends of your friends, visit nostr.directory.
